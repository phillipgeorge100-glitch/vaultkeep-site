Frequently asked questions

Where is my data stored?+
On your device. VaultKeep stores all portfolio data locally — in your browser or, for the desktop app, in an encrypted file on your machine. Nothing is transmitted to any server. You own your data completely.
Do I need to give VaultKeep my bank credentials?+
No. VaultKeep never asks for bank logins, API tokens, or account credentials. You add your holdings manually or import via CSV. This is a deliberate design choice — your credentials should never leave your bank.
How does the AI assistant work without sending data to VaultKeep?+
You provide your own API key from providers like OpenAI or Anthropic. When you ask a question, VaultKeep sends your query directly from your device to that provider. We are never in the middle, never see the conversation, and never store it.
What if I lose my device?+
VaultKeep includes JSON export for full backups. We recommend exporting regularly and storing backups securely. The desktop app stores data locally, so standard device backup practices apply.
What brokers can I import from?+
VaultKeep supports CSV import from major UK and international brokers including Hargreaves Lansdown, Vanguard, Trading 212, Interactive Investor, Coinbase, Binance, and Kraken. You can also use any custom CSV format.
Is VaultKeep regulated by the FCA?+
VaultKeep is a portfolio tracking tool, not a financial service. It does not provide investment advice, manage funds, or execute trades. All calculations are informational. You make your own decisions.
What is the difference between Free and Essential?+
Free gives you the full dashboard experience with up to 5 accounts and 20 holdings. Essential removes those limits, adds target allocation and rebalancing, financial projections, snapshot history, and the desktop app — for a single one-time payment.
