Frequently asked questions.

Where is my data stored? +
On your device. VaultKeep is a desktop app for Mac and Windows. All portfolio data lives in a local file on your machine. Nothing is transmitted to any VaultKeep server.
Do I need an internet connection? +
No. VaultKeep works fully offline. Price feeds and the AI assistant need internet when you choose to use them. The core app (your dashboard, calculations and portfolio) works without any connection.
Do I need EODHD or AI API keys? +
Both are optional. VaultKeep runs fully on data you enter yourself, like a refined spreadsheet. Every account, holding, asset, liability, projection and dashboard card works the same.

If you want live prices, the EODHD key is bring-your-own. EODHD's free tier covers a typical portfolio of 5 to 20 holdings refreshed once a day. Their All-World paid plan (around £19.99 per month, paid directly to EODHD) adds frequent refreshes, realtime quotes, extended history, and unlocks some advanced features including Watchlist live updates, auto-refreshing macro indicators, server-calculated trailing returns, and longer lookback on the Projections page.

If you want the AI assistant, you bring your own provider key (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, xAI, or DeepSeek). Many providers have generous free tiers.

In every case, the licences are yours, the bills go directly to the providers, and we never see your usage.
How does the AI assistant work without sending my data to VaultKeep? +
You provide your own API key from one of five supported providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, xAI, DeepSeek). When you ask a question, the desktop app sends your query straight to that provider. We are never in the middle, never see the conversation, and never store it.
What happens if I lose my device? +
VaultKeep includes JSON export for full backups. Set the auto-backup feature to save to your folder on a schedule, or export manually whenever you want. The desktop app stores data locally, so standard device backup practices also apply.
Is VaultKeep regulated by the FCA? +
VaultKeep is a portfolio tracking tool, not a financial service. It does not provide investment advice, manage client funds, or execute trades. All calculations are informational. You make your own decisions.
Can I use VaultKeep on multiple devices? +
The desktop app runs on macOS and Windows. Data is stored locally per device. To use VaultKeep on more than one machine, export a JSON backup from one and import it on the other. Your perpetual licence covers up to five devices.
